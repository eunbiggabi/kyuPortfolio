Babies get COVID-19 antibodies from mothers while in the womb, study findse

Antibodies that protect against the coronavirus often get transferred from mother to infant during pregnancy — a finding that indicates a mother passes along at least some protection to her child, researchers reported Friday. The findings also suggest that vaccinating pregnant women might protect their unborn babies, as well, the researchers said — and it's worth studying the possibility. Pregnant women are advised to get influenza vaccines to protect both themselves and their babies. Newborn specialist Dr. Dustin Flannery of Children's Hospital of Philadelphia and colleagues studied more than 1,400 mothers and newborns. They found that protective IgG antibodies were transferred across the placenta in 72 out of 83 infected or previously infected pregnant women they studied.Newborns are vulnerable to infection, and any protection would be welcome, Munoz wrote in an accompanying commentary.
